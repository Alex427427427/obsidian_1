A note on the Scandinavian languages. 

By many general criteria of mutual intelligibility, the Continental Scandinavian languages could be considered dialects of a common Scandinavian language. However, because of several hundred years of intense rivalry between Denmark and Sweden, including a long series of wars from the 16th to 18th centuries, and the nationalist ideas that emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the languages have separate orthographies, dictionaries, grammars and regulatory bodies. Danish, Norwegian, Swedish are thus from a linguistic perspective more accurately described as a dialect continuum of Scandinavian (North Germanic).
