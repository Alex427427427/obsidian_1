A language family. [[Language Families]]

Native to the northern Indian subcontinent, most of Europe, and the Iranian plateau. 

They descend from a single prehistoric language, linguistically reconstructed as Proto-Indo-European, spoken during the Neolithic (新石器时代) or early Bronze Age. 

### Major Branches of interest to the note taker
##### Germanic
English, German, Dutch, Scots, Danish, Swedish, Norwegian, Afrikaans (Dutch colony in South Africa)
##### Italic
Latin and its descendants - the Romance languages: French, Italian, Spanish. 
##### Slavic
Bulgarian, Russian, Polish, Czech, Slovak, Macedonian, Ukrainian, Belarusian. 
##### Hellenic
Only Greek. No close living relatives within this family.
