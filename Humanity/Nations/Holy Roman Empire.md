A polity in Central and Western Europe, headed by the Holy Roman Emperor. Developed in the Early Middle Ages (400-900, after Western Rome's fall). 

Defining ideology was that they were the one true continuation of the Roman Empire of Antiquity, and the Holy Roman Emperor was the leading monarch of Europe. 

Had been quite powerful throughout its long existence. 

Was recognised by most of Europe as a legitimate continuation of Rome, since the emperors were proclaimed to be Roman Emperors by the pope. The emperors claimed to be universal monarchs with jurisdiction over all of Christian Europe and beyond. 

Initially made of Germany, Italy and Burgundy. By Late Middle Ages (1300-1500), governance became concentrated in Germany. 

Dissolved after defeat in war by Napoleon in the early 1800s.
