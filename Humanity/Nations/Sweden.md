Formally known as the Kingdom of Sweden. Largest Nordic country. Capital Stockholm.

A highly developed country ranked 5th in the Human Development Index. A constitutional monarchy and a parliamentary democracy. A unitary state divided into 21 counties and 290 municipalities. 

Maintains a Nordic social welfare system that promises universal healthcare and tertiary education for its citizens. 

Has the world's 14th highest GDP per capita and ranks very highly in quality of life, health, education, protection of civil liberties, income equality and gender equality. 

Joined EU in 1995 and NATO in 2024.
### Demographic
10.6 M as of 2025. 
Mostly reside in the southern half. 
### Language
Swedish (North Germanic ([[Indo-European]]))

Co-official language in Finland.

### History
##### Prehistory up to 6th Century
Inhabited since around 12,000 BCE. Late Palaeolithic reindeer hunting camps. Small clans of hunter gatherers who relied on flint technology. 

The Geats and Swedes that emerged formed a part of the sea-faring peoples known as the Norsemen. 

First described by Roman historian Tacitus in his Germania (on the subject of the German peoples, 98CE), which mentioned the Swedes as a powerful tribe with strong fleets. 

Norse mythology presents a long line of legendary kings going back to the final centuries BCE. 

Byzantine historian mentioned them again in the 6th century. 
##### Viking Age 8th - 11th Century
Some carried out raids, some were invited to be the Byzantine Emperor's personal bodyguards, some went to found early states, like Kievan Rus. Described by many outside sources such as the Arab traveller Ibn Fadlan. 

The actions of these Vikings were commemorated on many runestones. 
##### Kingdom of Sweden
The actual age is unknown. Historians typically date this to the 10th Century. 

Feudalism never really developed in Sweden, unlike the rest of Europe. Peasantry remained largely a class of free farmers. Slavery was not common either. 

Christianity was introduced by a saint in the 8th Century. 
##### 1397-1523, Part of Kalmar Union
Sweden joined Norway and Denmark to form the Kalmar Union, which Sweden left in 1523. 
##### Swedish Empire
Sweden joined the [[1618 - 1648 Thirty Years' War]] on the Protestant side. Expanded territory as a result of the war. 

Rose to prominence on a continental scale during the reign of Gustavus Adolphus. Conquered about half of the Holy Roman Empire states and defeated the imperial army. Gustavus himself planned to become the Holy Roman Emperor, ruling over a united Scandinavia and Holy Roman states, but was killed in battle shortly after. Most German states left the Empire at the end of the Thirty Years' War, but Sweden still had much new territory conquered - 3rd largest nation in Europe. 

They remained one of the great powers of Europe until the early 18th century. 
##### Loss of Territory, 18th-19th Centuries
In a series of conflicts. e.g. Modern day Finland lost to Imperial Russia. 
##### Modern Age
Massive emigration to US in the 1850s to 1900s. 
Sweden was poor now, remaining agricultural economy instead of industrialising. 

Population increased massively despite slow industrialisation. Democratic movements converted Sweden into a parliamentary democracy by WWI. 

People started joining socialist movements, but a communist revolution was avoided in 1917. 
##### WWI and WWII
Neutral during WWI, but German Empire pressured them into doing small things that hindered the Allied Powers. Sweden provided volunteers to fight alongside Germans for the White Guards against the Red Guards and Russians in the Finnish Civil War. 

Neutral during WWII. Under German influence and ties to the rest of the world were cut through blockades. Helped rescue Jews. Supported Norwegian resistance against Germany. 
##### Post War
Still neutral, remained outside NATO and Warsaw Pact. But privately had strong ties with US and other Western govs. 

Had intact industrial base, social stability and natural resources after the war, so they supplied rebuilding of Europe. 

Governed by the Social Democratic Party with large cooperation with trade unions and industry. 

Slight economic decline due to the oil embargoes in the 70s. 
##### Recent History
Joined EU in 1995, but didn't adopt Euro. 

Joined NATO in 2024. 

Became more culturally diverse due to significant immigration. in 2013, about 15% population was foreign-born. Sweden was also affected by the 2015 European migrant crisis, forcing the government to righten regulations of entry into the country.

### The Nobel Prize
"for the greatest benefit to humankind"

First awarded in 1901, 5 years after Alfred Nobel's death. Originally covered 5 fields: physics, chemistry, physiology/medicine, literature, peace. In 1968, Economics was added. Each field is awarded annually. 

Amount approximately $1M USD as of 2025. 

Not awarded posthumously (after death). 

Alfred Nobel amassed a fortune during his lifetime for his 355 inventions. After reading an article about his own death (by mistake; the person who died was his brother), he became wary of how he would be remembered. This inspired him to change his will, which specified that his fortune be used to create a series of prizes for those who confer the greatest benefit on mankind. The executors of the will formed the Nobel Foundation. His will instructed a Norwegian Committee to award the Peace Prize. 

No prizes were awarded during some periods of WWII. 

Nomination forms are sent by the Nobel Committee to about 3000 people in the September before the year of the award. They are prominent academics working in a relevant area. The nominations are kept secret, not even known by the nominees themselves.
