The largest Christian sect. An entire world wide order. Headquartered in the Vatican City. 

Since the 20th Century the majority of them reside in the Global South (Africa, Asia, South America)

Most defining aspect: 
The Catholic church was founded directly by Jesus' apostles during the "great commission" - resurrected Jesus instructing his disciples to spread the gospel to all nations of the world. 
The pope is the successor of Saint Peter, (primacy apostle), and the bishops are the successors of Christ's apostles. 

Catholic = Greek for universal, world-wide, all inclusive. 

### Relationship to Roman Empire ([[Roman Empire of Antiquity]])
##### Early Hostility (1st - 3rd Centuries)
The early Christians were a minority sect within the Roman world. 
Rome demanded public loyalty to the emperor and the state religion. 
Periodic persecution. 
##### Legalisation and Imperial Adoption (4th Century)
Key figure: Constantine the Great, Roman Emperor from 306-337 CE.
Legalised Christianity and ended state persecution. Constantine converted to Christianity himself after a vision before battle ("In this sign, conquer").

The church gained privileges, property and political influence. 
The emperor became the church's protector, but not yet fully under its control. 
##### Christianity becomes the State Religion (late 4th Cntury)
Theodosius I (380 CE) declared Christianity the *official religion* of the Roman Empire. 
Pagan temples were closed or destroyed; rituals banned. 
The church now absorbed much of the Roman imperial structure, and became the moral and spiritual arm of the empire. 
##### Fall of the Western Empire (5th Century)
The Church outlasted the collapse of the Western Roman Empire. 
Bishops became the most stable authority figures in the West, especially the Bishop of Rome (the Pope). 
The Church preserved literacy, law and order - inheriting the imperial mantle. 

In the East (Byzantine Empire), the emperor maintained authority over the Church. 
##### The Church as Heir to Rome (Medieval era onward)
The Papacy (Pope) positioned itself as the spiritual successor of Rome's political power. 

The coronation of Charlemagne by the Pope in 800 CE symbolised this transfer. The [[Holy Roman Empire]] was a Christianised rebirth of Rome. 

The Church thus became both the child and the heir of the Roman Empire.
