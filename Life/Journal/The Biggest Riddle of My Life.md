___
The final riddle of my life is being chronically scared of ending up single. 

### How it Affects Me
To me, this outcome is so catastrophic that it arrests all my life decisions. 
It often makes me anxious. 

I've turned pair bonding into the ultimate verdict on my worth. Every job, move, haircut, breakup... all of it, is filtered through one question:
- Does this increase or decrease my probability of being chosen? 
This is about performance under existential evaluation. 
### Cause
The cause of it, evolution. And feeling fundamental instability and emotional neglect throughout childhood and adolescence. From an early age, home was not a safe place. 

### Closing Window. Really?
I'm living as though there is a narrow, closing window and a single wrong move ruins everything. This belief creates paralysis and anxiety. I'm overestimating how much individual decision determines the final outcome. The truth is that pair bonding is highly probabilistic. People meet through randomness, timing, relocation, failure, reinvention. 

### Break ups. Mistakes?
Every previous partner I move on from, kills what could have been my future. 

But compatibility isn't a rare gem I accidentally throw away. If a relationship ends, it ended because it wasn't sustainable under real conditions. Moving on doesn't kill the future but rather prevents a mismatched future. 

### Optimisation. Futile.
Trying to optimise life for selection:
- better job -> higher status -> higher attractiveness
- better looks -> higher desirability
- more time dating -> higher odds
But attraction can't be engineered like a physics problem. 

### Hair in the shower
A mundane event becomes existential:
- hair loss -> reduced attractiveness -> reduced chances -> lifetime alone -> catastrophic existential failure
But many partnered men are bald. Attraction is multi-dimensional. Confidence and emotional stability outweigh aesthetics. 

### Mindset
"I must not end up single at all costs". 
causes fear-based decisions and anxiety-driven attachment. People can sense when they are being treated as existential solutions. Love thrives when two stable people choose each other, not when one of them is trying to escape annihilation. 

### Why Perpetual Solitude is not Fatal
Fatal := a life so consistently painful that it would not be positively chosen. 

Humans adapt to almost any stable condition. Psychological baselines shift. People that lose spouses, mobility, careers, even limbs, often return close to previous well-being over time. The brain recalibrates. The mind telling me "I would not choose life under that condition" is imagining it from current attachment state to this topic, not from a future adapted state. 

### Why Being Unmarried is a Tolerable Outcome
It is a matter of fact that pair bonding is roughly orthogonal to life enjoyment in old age. Meaning that married people have about the same good and bad possibilities compared to unmarried people. What matters more is other areas of social life. 

Social integration, physical health, cognitive vitality, community role, financial security. 

Consider the widowed that are embedded in community. Childless but socially active. Single but purposeful. Consider the married trapped in resentment, emotionally distant, lonely beside someone. 

I need to build a life with redundancy - friends, community, meaning, an inner mind that doesn't translate the unmarried life into pain. 

### Why it is Realistic to Expect I will Probably Meet My Wife
The base rate for long term pair bonding in most developed societies is high. The majority of men eventually form long term relationships or marriages at some point in life. It is not guaranteed, but is statistically probable. 

What increases probability:
- continued exposure
- geographic mobility
- psychological stability
- social competence

### Existence and Annihilation
Is existence NOT evaluated on this criteria of pair bonding? That I have been deemed externally worthy of existing and replicating? Is it NOT the most important failure in life to not be able to pair bond?

At first, it seems that evolution optimises gene propagation, and not subjective meaning. 
But evolution also produced: 
- art
- physics
- music
- curiosity about the universe

If pure reproduction was the ultimate metric of value, then:
- a mediocre but fertile individual outperforms a childless scientist who expands human knowledge. 
**Is that your actual value system?**
I admire reason, science, progress. Those are not reducible to gene replication. 

If existence were solely about being chosen, then the infertile, the celibate monk, the lifelong researcher, the artist who never married - they all lived failed lives. 
**Is that your position?** 

Mate selection is not a cosmic tribunal. It's a messy, contingent, culturally shaped random process. In the modern age, above attractiveness and competence, it measures a whole lot of noise. It does not measure existential worth. 
### The Actionable Core
- Consistent social exposure
- staying in shape
- date intentionally
- build a life that is inherently meaningful. 
- Leave the rest to probability

### Truths
**I am not one wrong job away from permanent isolation. I am not one break up away from losing my only chance. I am not one head of hair away from becoming unlovable.**

**Perpetual solitude is not fatally painful because the brain adapts to any steady state external condition. Being unmarried is probably a tolerable outcome because it is roughly orthogonal to old age well-being. Aside from that, it is probable I will meet my wife as a statistical fact. But at the bottom of all this: pair bonding CANNOT be a cosmic tribunal. If it were, all the childless scientists and artists who expanded human knowledge and awareness lived failed lives.**
