1. **Nothing/no one external brings true happiness.**
2. Time is short. 
3. No one has it figured out. 
4. My deepest desire is to be **affirmed by another consciousness**
5. Don't wait to be happy. **Be happy right now, void of conditions.** Because the happiness that requires a condition does not exist. 

I think I am approaching an apprehension of life that aligns with most tested insights from real human experience. 
**This view of life, however, is terrifying for me, right now.**

The lack of real meaning, the lack of everlasting happiness, the shortness. This is scary. Life as we know it right now, is a scary affair. 80 years, we are so strained at the end, having striven for happiness by so many avenues, and finding not an ounce that lasts. Then suddenly, life is mercilessly terminated. 

### Counterargument
Consider someone offering you $1B. Would you take it? Yes, I would. 

Now consider someone offering you $1B, but you don't get to wake up tomorrow. Would you still take it? No, I wouldn't. 

It appears that getting to wake up tomorrow is worth more than $1B. **It appears that being alive is priceless.** 

It would take a tremendous amount of immediate suffering for this choice to flip. What is the line? Right now, the worst case of life I am imagining is one where no other consciousness acknowledges my own deeply, and I am near the end of my own life. A friendless old age. (Even in that scenario i could join writing groups etc but i digress.) It appears that as long as I am able-bodied and able-minded, waking up for one more day remains priceless. In other words, in the worst reasonable case scenario, living is still worth it. Though this is not a logically justifiable position a priori, but a visceral experimental fact.
