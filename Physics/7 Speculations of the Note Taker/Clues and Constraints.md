### Transversal Non-Locality, Longitudinal Locality
The quantum state is non-local in the transverse direction of propagation. 
The quantum state is local in the longitudinal direction of propagation. This is enforced by relativistic quantum mechanics. (QFT, Dirac Eqn) 

### Measurement Must be Able to "Push" the Entity AWAY from the Detector as Much as it is Able to Pull the Entity in
Consider the causal wavefront of the wavefunction propagating at the speed of light. A beamsplitter splits the wave into 2 paths of different lengths. A detector sits at the end of each. **If the collapse is a causal mechanism involving the detector and the wavefunction amplitude**, then this mechanism must be very strange indeed. It is not hard to imagine the amplitude interacting with the detector to produce a folding of the wavefront towards a location, even if the folding is non-local across the wavefront. However, that mechanism must also be capable of extinguishing the wavefront and redistributing the wave amplitude elsewhere in the wavefront. Otherwise, the quantum entity would never reach detector 2.
